## 1. HOW DEALERSHIP FINANCE IS PRICED

### The Traditional Dealer Finance Pricing Stack

Dealerships act as FCA-authorised credit brokers. The pricing waterfall works like this:

```
Consumer APR = Lender Base Rate + Dealer Commission Markup
```

**Pre-FCA DCA Ruling (pre-2021):**
- Dealers had **discretionary commission arrangements (DCAs)** — the higher the APR they placed the customer at, the more commission they earned
- A dealer could move a customer from a lender's "floor rate" of, say, 8% to 18%, earning the delta as commission
- Industry data (FCA 2019 review): DCAs resulted in ~£165 MORE interest per loan on average
- This created a structural incentive to push customers into higher-APR products

**Post-DCA Ruling (2021 onwards, increasingly enforced 2024):**
- FCA banned DCAs — dealers must now use flat-fee or fixed commissions
- Flat fee range: **£200–£800 per funded deal** (varies by lender, product, and loan size)
- Dealers can no longer inflate APRs to earn more; lenders set the consumer rate

### Typical APR Markup over Lender Cost of Funds (Estimated, 2024)

| Tier | Lender Cost of Funds | Typical Consumer APR | Approximate Margin |
|------|---------------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Prime new car (OEM subsidised) | 4%–6% | 0%–6.9% (OEM subsidises) | OEM absorbs — pays lender to offer 0% |
| Prime new car (unsubsidised) | 4%–6% | 7.9%–12.9% | 2–7% spread |
| Prime used car | 5%–7% | 9.9%–14.9% | 3–8% spread |
| Near-prime | 8%–12% | 14.9%–22.9% | 5–11% spread |
| Subprime | 12%–18% | 22.9%–34.9% | 8–15% spread |
| Deep subprime | 18%–25%+ | 34.9%–49.9% | 10–20%+ spread |

**Note:** Lender cost of funds includes their funding cost, credit loss provisioning, and operational costs. Margin above = gross interest revenue before servicing costs.

### Finance Penetration Rates by Segment (FLA 2024 Data)

- **New cars (franchise dealers):** 83% of private new car registrations financed via FLA members
  - PCP is dominant product type: ~80% of financed new cars
  - HP: ~15%; Leasing (PCH): ~5% of private buyers
- **Used cars (franchise dealers):** ~60–65% of used car sales go through dealer finance
- **Used cars (independent dealers):** ~35–45% penetration (lower — less finance infrastructure)
- **Online brokers (Zuto, CF247):** Specifically target the non-dealer-arranged segment — customers who want to arrange finance independently before visiting dealer

### New vs Used Car Finance Pricing Differences

| Factor | New Car | Used Car |
|--------|---------|----------|
| OEM subsidised rates | Common (0%–6.9%) | Rare / non-existent |
| Typical prime APR | 1.9%–9.9% | 7.9%–14.9% |
| Typical subprime APR | 15%–24.9% (uncommon) | 22.9%–49.9% |
| PCP availability | Standard | Available but limited on high-mileage/older |
| Lender risk view | Lower (new asset, clear valuation) | Higher (depreciation risk, age risk) |
| Finance penetration | 83% | 60–65% |
| Commission earned | Lower per-unit (subsidised deals = lower base) | Higher per-unit (more margin available) |

## 2. MARKETPLACE FINANCE PRICING DYNAMICS

### AutoTrader — The Aggregator Model

AutoTrader is not a lender or direct broker. It's an **advertising aggregator** that:
- Shows representative APR on each listing (provided by the retailer/lender)
- Offers its own finance referral service via Zuto and CarMoney (earns referral commission)
- Earns commission on finance applications — disclosed clearly (post-FCA requirement)

**AutoTrader's pricing influence:**
- None directly — APR is set by retailer/lender
- Indirectly creates **price transparency pressure**: buyers can compare APRs across listings
- This transparency is increasing consumer price sensitivity in prime segment
- Does NOT cover subprime: subprime buyers who click through to listed subprime dealers are served by those dealers' own lender panels

### CarWow — New Car Platform

- Finance is manufacturer-led: CarWow shows OEM-subsidised PCP rates
- 0% APR deals visible when manufacturers run promotions (seasonal, end-of-quarter)
- CarWow's finance "calculator" on configurator = lead gen for dealer's OEM finance
- **No subprime exposure** — entirely prime/super-prime market
- Pricing driven by OEM marketing budget, not dealer discretion

### Cinch — D2C Online Retailer

- Cinch sets its own finance terms via panel lenders
- Pricing philosophy: **convenience premium** — slightly above market APR, but bundled with free delivery, 14-day returns
- Near-prime is their ceiling: they do not serve deep subprime
- APR transparency: Cinch shows representative APR in listing — more visible than traditional dealer floor walk

## 3. KEY PRICING DYNAMICS AND TRENDS

### 3.1 The FCA DCA Ruling — Reshaping the Market

The discretionary commission arrangement ban + ongoing FCA investigation is the most significant structural shift in UK car finance pricing in a decade:

**Before 2021:**
- Dealers routinely added 3–8% to consumer APR above lender floor rate
- Consumer APR of 18% on a car might have a floor rate of 10%; dealer earned the 8% delta as commission
- Zero transparency to consumer on this markup

**After 2024 ruling:**
- Flat commission structure: dealers earn £200–£800 regardless of rate placed
- No incentive to push higher rates
- **Winner:** Consumers in dealer channel (lower APRs)
- **Winner:** Online brokers with transparent models (e.g., CF247, Zuto) — their model was already aligned with post-ruling standards
- **Loser:** Dealers and lenders who profited from DCAs; Close Brothers, Black Horse, Barclays under scrutiny

### 3.2 Interest Rate Environment Impact (2023–2025)

- Bank of England base rate peaked ~5.25% (2023), easing to ~4.25% by Q1 2026
- Lender cost of funds correspondingly elevated — prime consumer APRs rose 2–4% vs 2021
- Impact: PCP monthly payments on a £30k car rose ~£80–120/month at equivalent terms
- This **compressed subprime volume** as affordability squeezed — some borrowers exit the market
- Lenders tightened credit appetite 2022–2024; now easing slightly as rates fall

### 3.3 Subprime Pricing Power vs Volume Trade-off

From academic research (Stage 1) and market data:

| APR Band | Subprime Volume (estimated) | Default Rate | Net Revenue Yield |
|----------|----------------------------|--------------|------------------|
| 18%–24.9% | High | 4%–8% | Positive |
| 25%–34.9% | Medium | 8%–15% | Optimal range |
| 35%–44.9% | Lower | 15%–25% | Risk/reward peaks |
| 45%–49.9% | Low | 25%–35% | Marginal; cap here |

**Optimal subprime pricing zone: 24.9%–34.9% APR** — balances volume, default, and revenue
- Moneybarn, First Response operate at upper end
- Startline, Advantage operate at lower end (near-prime)
- 247 CarFinance's panel spans full spectrum

### 3.4 Transparency as Pricing Differentiator

Post-FCA ruling, **consumer awareness of APR is rising**. But still low at point of sale vs broker comparison:

- Dealer channel: APR often disclosed only in final F&I office conversation
- Online broker (CF247, Zuto): APR shown upfront in comparison; soft-search eligibility check
- Marketplace (AutoTrader): APR shown on listing but often "representative" not personalised

**247 CarFinance's opportunity:** Position as the "transparent" alternative — know your rate before you walk into a dealership. This is a clean marketing message in a post-DCA world.

## 5. PRICING STRATEGY RECOMMENDATIONS FOR 247 CF

### 5.1 Segment-Specific Rate Communication

**Prime segment (8.9%–14.9%):** Highlight speed, digital experience, and freedom to choose own car vs dealer ecosystem

**Near-prime (14.9%–22.9%):** "Most lenders won't help you — we can, and here's your actual rate before you shop"

**Subprime (22.9%–34.9%):** "We work with 20+ lenders to find you the lowest rate your credit allows" — emphasise panel breadth, not just access

**Deep subprime (34.9%+):** Position as "we'll find a way when others say no" — focus on access, not rate

### 5.2 Transparency as Product Feature

- **Show range, not just representative APR** — "your actual rate will be between X and Y based on your profile"
- Soft-search personalised rate without credit impact — now table stakes but 247 CF should make this prominent
- Comparison table (customer sees: "we searched 20 lenders; here are your 3 best offers")

### 5.3 Post-DCA Positioning ("Clean Finance")

- Lean into the FCA scandal: "We were never part of the commission scandal — our rates are always as low as we can get them"
- Target buyers who feel burned by dealer finance or are aware of the mis-selling claims
- This is a genuine differentiator that builds trust in a low-trust category

### 5.4 New Car Finance — Untapped Opportunity

- 247 CF primarily known for used car/subprime — new car finance is largely OEM-captive
- But: post-DCA era, prime buyers increasingly want to arrange finance independently
- 247 CF could expand panel to include prime new car lenders (Santander Consumer, Black Horse prime) and capture buyers who walk into a Lookers/VW dealer knowing their rate already
- "**Bring your finance to the dealership**" positioning — like mortgage pre-approval
