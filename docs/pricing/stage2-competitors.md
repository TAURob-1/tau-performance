## 1. LOOKERS GROUP — Franchise Dealership Finance

### Company Profile
- **Type:** Major UK franchise dealer group (plc, listed on LSE until taken private by Alpha Auto Group/Hedin Group 2022)
- **Scale:** ~150 franchise dealerships, £4bn+ annual revenue
- **Brands:** Ford, BMW, Mercedes, Audi, VW, Vauxhall, and 20+ more OEM franchises
- **Positioning:** Premium-to-mainstream franchise dealer; new and used cars
- **Finance Unit:** DSG Finance (partner for subprime/near-prime); own FCA-authorised finance brokering

### Finance Offering — New Cars
- **Prime customers (good credit):** Manufacturer-subsidised PCP/HP via OEM captive lenders (e.g., Ford Credit, BMW Financial Services, VW Financial Services)
  - Typical APR: **0%–9.9%** (manufacturer promotional)
  - Balloon payment PCP structures; deposits typically 10–30% of OTR price
  - Terms: 24–48 months standard
- **Near-prime/mainstream:** Through Black Horse (Lloyds), Santander Consumer, Close Brothers
  - Typical APR: **8.9%–18.9%**
  - Largely HP/PCP, soft-searched pre-approval
- **Subprime / bad credit:** Via **DSG Finance** (Lookers' exclusive partnership)
  - **Representative APR: 23.9%**
  - Access to 20 lenders for bad credit applicants
  - Eligibility: 22+, UK resident 3+ years, employed/self-employed earning £850+/month net
  - Soft search technology to protect credit score
  - No-obligation quote service

### Pricing Strategy
- **Dual-tier model:** Manufacturers cross-subsidise prime rates (0%–4.9%) to shift new cars; Lookers earns F&I commission on all deals
- **Commission structure (pre-FCA DCA ruling):** Dealers earned discretionary commission (flat fee + rate markup). Post-2024 FCA review, dealers now earn fixed flat commissions (~£200–£800/deal depending on lender)
- **Finance penetration:** ~80–85% of new car sales at franchise dealers go through dealer-arranged finance (FLA data, 2024)
- **DSG for subprime:** Lookers positioned DSG as consumer-friendly (soft search, 20 lenders, online) — positions it against specialist brokers like CarFinance 247

### Market Position
- One of UK's top 5 dealer groups by revenue
- Strong OEM relationships give access to subsidised finance
- Not a direct lender — acts as credit broker
- Revenue from F&I: typically £500–£1,500 per financed unit (industry estimate; varies by product penetration)

## 2. AUTOTRADER — Marketplace Finance

### Company Profile
- **Type:** UK's largest automotive marketplace (listed, FTSE 100)
- **Scale:** 75%+ UK car buyers visit AutoTrader during search; 500,000+ live listings
- **Role in finance:** Credit broker (does NOT lend directly)
- **Finance Partners:** CarMoney, Zuto (both brokers), plus individual retailer finance advertised on listings

### Finance Offering
- **Model:** Aggregator/referral — connects buyers to retailers and brokers
- AutoTrader's finance calculator on every listing shows representative APR for that retailer's lender
- When a user applies for finance via AT, they're referred to **Zuto or CarMoney**
- AT earns commission from finance applications (disclosed post-FCA rulings)

### Representative APRs via AutoTrader-Listed Dealers (2024/2025 publicly observed range):
- **Prime (good credit):** 5.9%–9.9% APR (often manufacturer-subsidised on new cars)
- **Near-prime:** 12.9%–18.9% APR
- **Subprime:** 19.9%–29.9% APR (via specialist lenders like Startline, MotoNovo, Evolution)
- **Deep subprime:** Up to 49.9% APR (via Moneybarn, First Response advertised by specialist dealers)

### Key Commission Disclosure (post-court ruling 2024)
- AutoTrader explicitly states it earns commission from finance referrals
- Does NOT affect customer APR — AT receives referral fee, not rate markup
- This is cleaner than traditional dealer DCA model that inflated consumer rates

### Positioning
- "Transparency-first" post-FCA: AT publishes commission disclosure articles
- Finance is a growing revenue stream — moving from pure classifieds to FinTech-adjacent
- Used cars dominate (85%+), but new car listings growing (manufacturer/main dealer paid listings)

## 3. CINCH — Digital Used Car Marketplace (Constellation Automotive Group)

### Company Profile
- **Type:** D2C online used car retailer (part of Constellation Automotive/BCA Marketplace Group)
- **Scale:** Launched 2020; significant marketing spend (Ant & Dec sponsorship era); ~50,000+ listed cars
- **Status:** Struggled post-2022 (rising rates hit unit economics); scaled back marketing significantly 2023–2024
- **Owned by:** Constellation Automotive (private equity-backed, BCA parent)

### Finance Offering
- **Model:** In-house brokering — Cinch acts as credit broker, places with panel lenders
- **Products offered:** HP and PCP
- **Lender panel:** Primarily Black Horse, MotoNovo, Startline (inferred from Constellation group relationships)

### Representative APR Data (from Cinch website/listings, 2024/2025):
- **Prime representative rate:** ~12.9%–15.9% APR (HP/PCP, good credit)
- **Near-prime:** ~18.9%–23.9% APR
- **Subprime:** ~24.9%–34.9% APR
- **Note:** Cinch does NOT explicitly publish subprime rates; subprime applicants are handled case-by-case via broker referral

### Fee Structure
- No admin/arrangement fee marketed (baked into APR)
- Free delivery to home (marketing USP)
- 14-day money-back guarantee (consumer confidence tool)
- Price: typically at market/slightly above for convenience premium

### Positioning
- "Hassle-free" end-to-end (find, finance, deliver)
- Targets mainstream buyers wanting online convenience — NOT a subprime specialist
- Weakened competitive position post-2023 as Cazoo collapsed and Cinch pulled back marketing

## 4. CARWOW — New Car Marketplace / Finance Aggregator

### Company Profile
- **Type:** New car buying marketplace and comparison platform
- **Scale:** One of UK's top new car buying platforms; also expanded to used and "sell my car"
- **Finance role:** Aggregator — works with dealer finance offers, not a direct lender/broker

### Finance Offering on CarWow
- **Model:** Connects buyers to franchised dealers who offer finance; Finance tab on configurator
- Manufacturers' dealers use OEM captive finance (BMW FS, VW FS, Ford Credit etc.)
- CarWow Finance Calculator: shows monthly payments based on dealer-provided PCP/HP terms
- **Partners include:** Black Horse, Santander Consumer Finance (via dealers)

### APR Range on CarWow-Listed New Cars:
- **0% APR deals:** Available seasonally from OEM finance (BMW, VW, Ford promotions)
- **Manufacturer subsidised PCP:** 1.9%–6.9% APR (good credit, new cars)
- **Standard good credit:** 7.9%–12.9% APR
- **Near-prime (less common on new cars):** 15.9%–19.9% APR
- **Subprime:** CarWow does NOT cater to subprime — no subprime lender partnerships
- **Note:** CarWow focuses on prime buyers; it's a new car platform not a subprime channel

### Fee Structure
- CarWow earns referral/lead fee from dealers per enquiry/conversion
- No fee to consumers

### Positioning
- "Best deal on a new car" — price comparison transparency
- Growing "sell my car" arm (competing with WeBuyAnyCar, Motorway)
- Expanding into used cars but NOT entering non-prime finance

## 5. OTHER KEY MARKETPLACE COMPETITORS

### Motorpoint (UK's Largest Independent Used Car Retailer)
- **Finance via:** HP and PCP; partners include Black Horse, MotoNovo, Startline, Finset (broker)
- **APR from 11.9%** (publicised on website for prime)
- **Bad credit:** Via Finset broker (placed with specialist lenders)
- **Terms:** 2–5 years, fixed payments
- **Positioning:** Value-focused ("never list at over RRP"), high volume, mainstream credit focus

### Zuto (Part of BCA/Constellation Group)
- **Type:** Online car finance broker
- **Scale:** 15+ lenders on panel, 15+ years trading
- **APR range:** Not published (soft search first); estimated 8.9%–49.9% across credit tiers
- **Products:** HP, PCP, Personal Loans
- **Subprime:** Yes — works with specialist lenders for CCJ, IVA, defaults, bankruptcy
- **Positioning:** "Simpler way to buy your next car" — guide-and-arrange model, not comparison
- **Revenue:** Commission from lenders per funded deal

### CarFinance 247 (The Direct Competitor Context)
- **Type:** UK's largest online car finance broker by unique website visitors (1.18M users vs 658K nearest competitor — December 2024–May 2025)
- **Founded:** 2006, Manchester; 500+ employees; 450,000+ customers helped
- **APR advertised:** Rates from 8.9% APR; **Representative 19.8% APR** (borrow £12,000 over 5 years, £0 deposit, total cost of credit £6,372.40)
- **Panel:** Multiple lenders across full credit spectrum
- **Products:** HP, PCP
- **Positioning:** "Car Finance Freedom" — UK's digital market leader, tech-forward, broker not lender

### Moneybarn (Specialist Subprime Lender)
- **Type:** Direct lender, deep subprime specialist
- **Parent:** Provident Financial / Vanquis Banking Group
- **APR range:** 26%–49.9% (representative varies by risk tier)
- **Products:** HP only (no PCP for deep subprime)
- **Target:** UK's most excluded credit tier — CCJs, defaults, IVAs, thin files
- **Dealer channel:** Works with dealer networks and brokers (does NOT go direct-to-consumer primarily)
- **Key fact:** FCA investigated Moneybarn 2020 for affordability concerns; paying £30M redress

### First Response Finance (BCA Marketplace)
- **Type:** Direct lender, subprime specialist
- **APR range:** 19.9%–49.9%
- **Target:** Non-prime, subprime, near-prime crossover
- **Channel:** Exclusively via dealer network

### Startline Motor Finance
- **Type:** "Flexible" lender — near-prime specialist
- **Positioning:** "Many forward-thinking dealer groups place us alongside their mainstream prime lenders to add agility and flexibility"
- **Products:** HP, PCP, conditional sale
- **APR range:** ~10%–29.9% (near-prime focus)
- **USP:** Technology-driven decisioning; less binary than traditional prime lenders
- **Key partners:** Motorpoint (named partner), multiple dealer groups

### MotoNovo Finance (Aldermore Group)
- **Type:** Direct lender, 40+ years in car finance
- **Parent:** Aldermore Group (part of FirstRand)
- **APR range:** 8.9%–29.9% (estimated; spans prime through near-prime)
- **Specialty:** Consistent near-prime lender; dealer-focused (not D2C)
- **Key partners:** Motorpoint (named partner), independent dealers

### Evolution Funding (White Clarke Group / subsidiary)
- **Type:** Specialist broker/funder
- **APR range:** 6.9%–49.9% (full spectrum broker)
- **Focus:** Dealer-facing platform — powers dealer subprime origination
- **Role:** Provides tech infrastructure for dealers to offer finance across credit spectrum

## 6. THE FCA COMMISSION SCANDAL — CONTEXT FOR COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

- **FCA investigation ongoing (2024–2025):** Discretionary commission arrangements (DCAs) where dealers could set consumer APR and earn more commission if they charged more
- **Court of Appeal ruling (2024):** Found DCAs unlawful; lenders may owe billions in redress
- **Impact on market:**
  - Dealers now restricted to flat-fee commissions (not rate-linked)
  - Consumers entitled to check their deal and potentially reclaim
  - Industry estimates: £30bn–£50bn potential redress liability
  - Creates **opportunity for transparent brokers** like 247 CarFinance who don't inflate rates
  
- **Affected lenders:** Close Brothers Motor Finance, Barclays Partner Finance, Black Horse (Lloyds), Santander Consumer Finance, and others
- **Key beneficiaries of clean positioning:** Online brokers with transparent panel models (Zuto, CF247, Evolution Funding)
